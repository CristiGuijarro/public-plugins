The capuchin (Cebus capucinus) is a medium-sized New World monkey of the family Cebidae. Native to the forests of Central America and the extreme north-western portion of South America, the capuchin is important to rainforest ecology for its role in dispersing seeds and pollen.

Capuchins are considered among the most intelligent of the New World monkeys; they have been the subject of many studies on behaviour and intelligence. They are also long-lived, with a maximum recorded age of over 54 years.

The ocean sunfish or common mola (Mola mola) is the heaviest known bony fish in the world, weighing between 247 and 1,000 kg as adults. The species is native to tropical and temperate waters around the globe. A member of the order Tetraodontiformes, which also includes pufferfish, porcupinefish, and filefish, the sunfish shares many traits common to members of this order.

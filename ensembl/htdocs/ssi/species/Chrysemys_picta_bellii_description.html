The painted turtle (Chrysemys picta) lives in slow-moving fresh waters from southern Canada to Louisiana and northern Mexico, and from the Atlantic to the Pacific. It is the only species of the genus Chrysemys, which is part of the pond turtle family Emydidae.

While habitat loss and road killings have reduced the turtle's population, its ability to live in human-disturbed settings has helped it remain the most abundant turtle in North America. Adults in the wild can live for more than 55 years.
